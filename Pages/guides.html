Travel Guides

Itineraries

Here are some of our popular itineraries!

New York City

What to see:
  • Empire State Building
  • Statue of Liberty
  • Rockerfeller Center
  • Central Park
  • MoMa
  • Times Square
  • Brooklyn Bridge
Where to eat:
  • 7th Street Burger
  • Los Tacos
  • Prince St. Pizza
  • Ruby's
  • The Osprey
  • Di Fara Pizza
  • Eataly
When to go:

New York offers a wide range of experiences depending on what season you visit! During the spring the city parks are in full bloom! The summer is perfect for exploring those outdoor attractions like Central Park and Coney Island! During the fall the foliage in the city's parks becomes beautifully colored, especially in Central Park! And during the winter you will see iconic events and decorations, like the Rockefeller Center Christmas Tree

What to bring:

For this trip there are a few important things. Make sure to bring comfortable walking shoes and appropriate clothing for the season you are visiting in. Other honorable mentions are a reusable water bottle, umbrella / rain jacket, a bag to carry around, a portable charger and a contactless payment method if you plan to use the public transportation.

Paris

What to see:
  • Eiffel Tower
  • Louvre Museum
  • Notre-Dame Cathedral
  • Palace of Versailles
Where to eat:
  • Le Jules Verne
  • LE Meurice Alain Ducasse
  • L'Atelier de Joel Robuchon Etoile
  • Bouillon Pigalle
When to go:

The most common time to visit Paris is during the summer, which brings large crowds and higher prices. If those don't seem too appealing to you, don't worry! The spring and the fall are some of the best times to visit Paris! During the spring you can expect warmer weather and blooming wildlife. You can also expect to see various outdoor concerts and festivals! During the fall you can expect cooler, crisp weather. The Luxembourg Gardens are beautiful this time of year. The fall also has various events including Paris Fashion Week and the Nuit Blanche art festival!

What to bring:

If you're traveling internationally to Paris there are a few necessities for a good vacation! France uses type E power sockets, so make sure you have the right adapters for your devices! It might also be beneficial to bring a French phrasebook or have a language app handy on your smartphone. Speaking of a smartphone, if you are worried about losing your documents, keep a copy of them separate from the originals in case of loss or theft. Finally, it never hurts to have a map handy!

Stockholm

What to see:
  • Gamla Stan (Old Town)
  • Vasa Museum
  • Djurgården
  • Fotografiska
  • Stadshuset (City Hall)
  • Humlegården (Park)
Where to eat:
  • Luca Pizza
  • Farang
  • Östermalmshallen
  • Meatballs for the people
  • Urban Deli
  • Lillebrors bageri
When to go:

The best time to visit Stockholm is in the summer or spring (April to August). Summer is ideal for exploring outdoor attractions and enjoying festivals and outdoor dining. In the summer there are also several ferrys that can take you to the small islands in the archipelago. Spring and autumn offer mild temperatures, perfect for city strolls. Winter (November to March) can be cold and snowy, but it's a magical time to experience Swedish traditions like ice skating, Christmas markets, and cozy candlelit cafes.

What to bring:

Sweden uses type E power sockets, so make sure you have the right adapters for your devices! Cash is not King in Sweden, so you don't need to bother about bringing cash. If you are visiting in winter, you need to bring a warm jacket, hat and gloves. An umbrella and a resuable bag is also recommended.

Melbourne

What to see:
  • Federation Square
  • Queen Victoria Market
  • Royal Botanic Gardens
  • Melbourne Zoo
  • St. Kilda Beach
Where to eat:
  • Chin Chin
  • Movida
  • Attica
  • Hardware Société
  • Supernormal
  • Hardware Société
When to go:

Melbourne experiences four distinct seasons. Spring (September to November) and Autumn (March to May) are particularly pleasant with mild temperatures and colorful foliage. Summer (December to February) can get hot, but it's great for outdoor activities and festivals. Winter (June to August) can be chilly, but indoor attractions and cozy cafes thrive during this time.

What to bring:

Make sure to bring comfortable walking shoes and layers of clothing, suitable for Melbourne's changeable weather. If you are visiting in summer, it can be good to bring a reusable water bottle, sunscreen and sunglasses. If you are going in spring or autumn, an umbrella or rain jacket is recomended. A must to bring is a camera or smartphone to capture all the vibrant street art and scenic landscapes.
