Itineraries

Here are some of our popular itineraries!

New York City

What to see:
  • Empire State Building
  • Statue of Liberty
  • Rockerfeller Center
  • Central Park
  • MoMa
  • Times Square
  • Brooklyn Bridge
Where to eat:
  • 7th Street Burger
  • Los Tacos
  • Prince St. Pizza
  • Ruby's
  • The Osprey
  • Di Fara Pizza
  • Eataly
When to go:

New York offers a wide range of experiences depending on what season you visit! During the spring the city parks are in full bloom! The summer is perfect for exploring those outdoor attractions like Central Park and Coney Island! During the fall the foliage in the city's parks becomes beautifully colored, especially in Central Park! And during the winter you will see iconic events and decorations, like the Rockefeller Center Christmas Tree

What to bring:

For this trip there are a few important things. Make sure to bring comfortable walking shoes and appropriate clothing for the season you are visiting in. Other honorable mentions are a reusable water bottle, umbrella / rain jacket, a bag to carry around, a portable charger and a contactless payment method if you plan to use the public transportation.

Paris

What to see:
  • Eiffel Tower
  • Louvre Museum
  • Notre-Dame Cathedral
  • Palace of Versailles
Where to eat:
  • Le Jules Verne
  • LE Meurice Alain Ducasse
  • L'Atelier de Joel Robuchon Etoile
  • Bouillon Pigalle
When to go:

The most common time to visit Paris is during the summer, which brings large crowds and higher prices. If those don't seem too appealing to you, don't worry! The spring and the fall are some of the best times to visit Paris! During the spring you can expect warmer weather and blooming wildlife. You can also expect to see various outdoor concerts and festivals! During the fall you can expect cooler, crisp weather. The Luxembourg Gardens are beautiful this time of year. The fall also has various events including Paris Fashion Week and the Nuit Blanche art festival!

What to bring:

If you're traveling internationally to Paris there are a few necessities for a good vacation! France uses type E power sockets, so make sure you have the right adapters for your devices! It might also be beneficial to bring a French phrasebook or have a language app handy on your smartphone. Speaking of a smartphone, if you are worried about losing your documents, keep a copy of them separate from the originals in case of loss or theft. Finally, it never hurts to have a map handy!
